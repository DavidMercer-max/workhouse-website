Customer Relationship Management — the clue is in the name, but brands often forget the customer should sit at the heart of any effective CRM strategy. Too often clients see CRM as a channel for pumping out products, saying what they want to say rather than what the customer wants to hear. Couple that with scatter-gun scheduling, no segmentation and a lack of data insight, and you switch customers off rather than building relationships.

A well-engineered CRM strategy is a vital cog for long-term brand building. Done well, it reinforces brand messaging at every touch point, enhances experience, connects communities and deepens relationships — driving loyalty and advocacy. It should never be about 'sell, sell, sell!'.

**Our core principle when building CRM strategies is relevancy.** Brands that do CRM well cut through the noise with content that's engaging but, most importantly, actually useful. We always start with the customer — flooding planning sessions with outside perspective (macro trends, topicality, content anchors, customer mindsets, barriers and opportunities) before developing comms that support their experience at any stage of the journey.

Data plays a big role — the more we know, the more relevant we can be, which is why segmentation is vital for brands with diverse offerings and fragmented audiences. Contextual relevance matters too: internal feeds (like stock data) can update product content live; external APIs and geo-location can serve content based on when and where customers receive comms.

A robust measurement and analysis framework is vital. We run regular measurement sessions with clients — deep dives into performance to ascertain creative effectiveness and build testing plans. Detailed analysis optimises performance and justifies spend, so KPIs should be defined from the outset and tailored to each communication. The goal: a cycle of continual improvement where performance data informs strategy, which informs content and creative — analysing, testing and learning every cycle.
