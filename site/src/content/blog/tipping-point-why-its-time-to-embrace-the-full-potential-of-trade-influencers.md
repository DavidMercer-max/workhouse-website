Influencer marketing isn't new — 75% of B2B marketers now use influencers in their mix. But most are only scratching the surface of the potential for brands in the built environment.

**Why trade influencers matter** — Trade influencers were borne out of a specific need: connection in a world where many tradespeople work in isolation for long stretches. Most acute among younger trades, but older tradespeople increasingly use Instagram and Facebook to stay connected and learn. By building public profiles and communities, they share knowledge and showcase skills. That peer-to-peer validation is worth 100x more than any advert — especially for younger, sceptical audiences.

**Making it meaningful** — Performance means everything. Unlike consumer influencers, any trade influencer worth their salt won't take cash from just anyone — their professional reputation hinges on who they work with. Get to know them, understand the products they use day-in day-out, and offer creative control so they can work with you authentically.

**Cutting the strings** — Cover yourself with a proper agreement, but don't dictate the specifics of every piece of content. It needs to ring true to their public persona. This builds brand credibility through the voices of people who use your products every day. Treat them as collaborators and ambassadors, not contractors.

**From strength to strength** — Platforms are evolving and peer-to-peer networks are becoming more powerful. Bricklaying has influencers with 100k+ Instagram followers and its own championship, Super Trowel — positioned more like a gaming convention than a trade event. Massive growth across plumbing, carpentry, and painting & decorating too.

**So, what's next?** — Let go of a top-down marketing mindset and embrace collaboration. The question is no longer whether trade influencers matter, but whether your brand is ready to treat them as true partners.

**Key Considerations:** The right partners (already love your products) · Take your time (understand their audience and tone) · Collaboration is key (tools and pointers, not scripts) · Rethink measurement (track referrals, product adoption, community sentiment) · Think long-term (real partnerships build authenticity over time).
