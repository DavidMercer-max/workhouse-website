Darren Clanford & Michael Rogan, co-founders of Material Source Studio, introduce their concept changing the future of built-environment and commercial interior specification. A three-minute walk from Manchester's Victoria Station, at 1 Federation Street in the Grade II listed Hanover Building, Material Source Studio is the first of its kind offering a holistic offline/online experience for architects, designers, makers and property professionals.

**A holistic experience** — In 2018, creative director Darren Clanford co-founded materialsource.co.uk, an online resource celebrating materials and the people who bring them to life, always planning to translate it into an immersive real-world experience. Michael brought operational expertise; both agreed the first site had to be Manchester — "an incomparable irreverent confidence that we want to harness." The doors opened in 2021 to a ready-and-waiting community craving tactility and collaboration after lockdown.

**A creative toolbox** — "A toolbox for the North West — a place where the architecture and design community can meet, share ideas and collaborate" (Michael Rogan). Across two levels: exhibits from 40+ leading brands, an 80-seat theatre, CPD area, 300+ book library, 7 meeting spaces, a co-working hub, a podcast studio, an internal garden with a 6-metre tree, and a seasonally-changing gallery.

**Doing things differently** — Commercial director David Smalley: "What we're offering is exponential value for our community. As we enter a new world where we do things differently, why not make things better than before?" Endorsed by visitors including Monika Swindells (Monika Studio) and Stevie Leigh (Fairhursts Design Group).

**What's next?** — With Manchester gaining momentum, attention turns to next destinations. Architects and designers are more time-poor than ever and keen to experience what's new, efficiently and sustainably. Material Source Studio is a physical response to 30+ years of direct conversation with architects and designers.
