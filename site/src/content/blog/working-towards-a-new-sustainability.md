A perfect storm — the pandemic, climate change, Brexit, social inequalities — has fundamentally shifted customer perspective and motivations. Built Environment organisations are in the eye of the storm: supply chain disruption, skilled labour shortages, shrinking margins, and now increasing accountability on environmental and community impact. Now is not the time for poignant posts and carefully selected hashtags — brands need to dig deeper. A **5-in-5 interview** with Regenerative Provocateur and Consultant **Martin Brown**:

- **Q1 (urgency):** We're witnessing weather patterns we thought belonged to the future, at only 1.2°C warming. Action has to be a 'whole of society' response — no one can opt out. Advice for brands: 'Act Now'. For our sector, a significant contributor to climate change, it's non-negotiable.
- **Q2 (is a Sustainability Policy enough?):** The 1987 Brundtland definition of sustainability has failed to arrest climate and ecological collapse. Words like eco, sustainable and green have become empty, enabling greenwashing. Question whether your actions are degenerative or regenerative.
- **Q3 (making the difference):** Regenerative thinking won't give a magic formula but enables better questions — shifting from product sustainability to a regenerative brand culture, focusing on potential not just problems, so organisations thrive as part of, not apart from, natural living systems.
- **Q4 (demonstrating commitment):** Be transparent; link to existing reporting, verify through ISO 14001, B Corp or similar. Product transparency via the Declare label (International Living Future Institute) — the 'food label' equivalent for the built environment.
- **Q5 (best advice):** Develop a regenerative culture — incremental improvements without shying from transformational action. One of the biggest opportunities we've ever had, needing authentic passion, creativity, disruption and real leadership.

**A view from the Brand Experts — Nicky Jepson, Marketing Director:** *The Re-generation of Brands.* Successful brands are only as good as their relevance, and climate change is now non-negotiable and a key purchase driver.
- **Get perspective** — Loyalties are only as good as your relevancy right now. Best aesthetic design won't save a product that can't be recycled. Data only takes you so far — walk a mile in your customers' shoes.
- **Think beyond sustainability** — Sustainability alone won't cut it. Genuinely regenerative brands are already ahead — eye on the future, long-term brand equity over short-term profit, investing in innovation and creative leadership.
- **Be authentically passionate** — Martin (spoke at COP26): "Be clear and accountable about your claims and intentions." Customers, future workforce and stakeholders are experts at sniffing out authenticity. An opportunity to stand up and be counted, not tick a 'sustainable' box.
