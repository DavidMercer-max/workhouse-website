There's a perception that B2B marketing is a little boring and beige compared to the bright lights of B2C — and the research backs the hunch. System1, with LinkedIn's B2B Institute, tested 1,600 B2B ads across 6m people and found 77% score 1 out of 5 for creative effectiveness (vs around half of B2C ads). Yet 20 years of IPA effectiveness case studies show emotionally-led creative drives more positive business effects than rational advertising — and still B2B marketers are almost twice as likely to rely on 'rational', product-centric creative. This is driven partly by B2B's focus on short-term sales: LinkedIn data shows B2B marketers spend only ~8% of budgets on long-term brand awareness.

B2B organisations are rarely marketing-led — sales or technical teams shout loudest, and the belief that product trumps brand means creativity gets dumped. Yet distinctive, entertaining and emotionally-led creative makes customers more likely to pay attention, purchase, and pay a premium.

**Distinctiveness** — Mental availability: distinctive, memorable creative is more effective because only ~5% of a B2B audience are in-market at any one time. When customers are eventually in the market, they gravitate to brands they remember. The brand that gets remembered is the brand that gets bought — and you only get remembered by being distinctive. Deliver wallpaper and you don't.

**Entertaining** — Humour works. It makes a brand relatable, demonstrates empathy, humanises, and makes an ad more memorable. Kantar's AdReaction study shows humour is the most powerful creative enhancer of receptivity; half their Creative Effectiveness Award winners used humour. 48% of B2B decision makers find B2B advertising boring, while 82% wish it had the creativity of B2C. "Humour doesn't just help a brand stand out, it gets you in the gut." (Kantar)

**Emotionally-led** — We underestimate the emotion tied up in B2B decisions. Harvard Business School has shown 95% of decision-making is subconscious. For B2B decision-makers the stakes are personal and high — get a procurement decision wrong and the consequences are far greater than a bad snack. "Emotions are the fastest way to deepen memory structures that guide how we make purchasing decisions." (Kantar)

**Up for the challenge?** — There's a clear link between creativity and effectiveness; most B2B businesses just haven't caught up. Getting stakeholders to buy into creativity isn't easy — the 'product delusion' is widespread. Ultimately it's our job as a creative agency to point marketing teams in the right direction and help them make the case for creativity.
